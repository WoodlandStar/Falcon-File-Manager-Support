Falcon File Manager — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 24 June 2026

Falcon File Manager (Falcon, the app, we) is a private, on-device media vault. Privacy is the core of how the app is designed.

We do not collect your data

Falcon has no servers and no user accounts. We do not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal information or any of your files. Nothing imported or created is sent externally.

Your files stay on your device

All media, documents, folders, and settings you add to Falcon are stored locally on your device. The app contains no cloud sync and never uploads content to external services. Falcon cannot access your files.

Regarding device backups: content may be included in standard iOS backups (iCloud Backup or an encrypted computer backup) if enabled in your device settings. These backups are Apple-encrypted and controlled by you, not Falcon. To exclude the vault from iCloud Backup, disable this feature in iOS Settings.

Permissions

Falcon may request these permissions, used only locally:

  • Photos — importing photos and videos into the vault
  • Camera and Microphone — capturing media directly into the app
  • Local Network — discovering nearby devices and transferring files over your Wi-Fi
  • Face ID / Touch ID — unlocking protected folders on your device

No tracking or analytics

Falcon contains no advertising, no analytics, and no third-party tracking.

Purchases

In-app purchases are handled entirely by Apple. Payment details are never received by Falcon.

Children

Falcon does not knowingly collect information from anyone, including children.

Changes

This policy may be updated periodically, with the revised date posted above.
